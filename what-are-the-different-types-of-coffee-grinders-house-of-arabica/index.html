    The Basics of Coffee Grinding 
    ​(And How It Works)

    Coffee grinding is the process of extracting the nutrients from the coffee beans.

    Several approaches may be used to accomplish this feat. People often mean coffee bean grinders by calling them coffee mills!

    I narrowed down to the electric and the manual coffee grinding machines as the two most outstanding ones.

    Electric coffee grinding is the simplest and by far, the more effective method.

    This method requires you to simply place the coffee beans inside the coffee grinders. You then have to switch on the grinder.

    And in a matter of minutes; the coffee is ground.

    There are two main kinds of electric coffee grinders; namely the blade grinder and the burr coffee grinder, respectively.

    Blade grinder works by chopping the beans into smaller sizes progressively until a smaller grain size cannot be attained.

    A burr grinder, on the other hand, works by squeezing the beans into finer particles.

    Of the two kinds of grinders, the burr grinder is the better.

    That’s because it achieves finer grain sizes and also holds accommodates more beans at a time.

    It is, however, more expensive than the blade grinder.

    More About Burr Coffee Grinders

    A burr coffee bean grinder comprises two revolving abrasive surfaces that grind the coffee beans into smaller and finer grains.

    A typical burr coffee mill consists of two burrs.

    One of the burrs is fixed onto the grinder’s body and attached to a threaded carrier.

    The other burr is fixed to the motor.

    How A Burr Coffee Grinder Works?

    When you switch on, the two burrs basically tear the coffee into smaller and tinier grains.

    The abrasive actions of the burrs are the ones that do this task of tearing the beans into tinier grain sizes.

    The grinder continues it crushing job, progressively until the coffee beans become as fine as powder.

    Once the powder has been generated, the rotating paddle of the machine pushes it through a chute and into a doser.

    Unlike the blade grinders, the burr grinder allows you to adjust appropriate texture and size of your coffee grinds.

    Types of Burr Coffee Grinders: 
    (Based on Burr Types)

    Many people I have seen only focus on nothing but the burr types.

    And it is true that – burr coffee grinders are further availed in several types and natures.

    This is a fact that many do not just seem to realize.

    Flat vs. Conical Burrs

    Shape of Burrs

    Flat burrs comprise two rings of burrs that sit at a horizontal elevation to the ground level.

    One burr faces up whereas; the remaining burr faces the downward trajectory.

    On the other hand, Conical burrs, contains one cone-shaped ring, inside another cone-shaped ring which in turn is hollow.

    Means of Grinding

    Flat burrs grind coffee by means of dropping the coffee beans in between the burrs.

    It crashes the coffee beans and then drops again repeatedly.

    The coffee makes 90-degree turn at each grind.

    Conical burrs, direct the coffee beans downwards through the burrs at some slight inclination (less than 90 degrees).

    It crashes the coffee beans and re-directs those repeatedly until the grounds attain the desired texture.

    Quality of Final Output

    In all, a conical burr coffee grinder produces a higher quality of coffee compared to the flat burrs.

    Actually, the conical shape enables all the coffee to be reached.

    More About Flat Burr Coffee Grinders

    Apart from its different kinds or types, there are a lot more to be known about burr grinders.

    I devote the rest of the sub-section to this.

    How does a Flat Burr Coffee Grinder Work?

    Grinding of beans by the flat burr grinder happens in three main stages.

    These are the bean breaking, the coarse grinding, and the fine grinding, respectively.

    You pour the coffee beans into the burrs.

    The machine then channels them through some constricted spaces inside the flat burrs.

    The burrs also rotate and squeeze out the coffee beans and pass them on to the other portions of the grinder.

    You then obtain finer and finer particles this way.

    Why Flat Burr Coffee Grinder is useful and/or preferred?

    Flat burrs are generally preferred for their simplicity i.e. they are generally easier to master and operate.

    As such, any user will find them convenient to utilize.

    You do not need to become a master and technical expert to operate them effectively.

    More About Conical Burr Coffee Grinders

    The conical grinder is almost uniform in shape, and as such allows all the coffee beans to be ground uniformly.

    How does a Conical Burr Coffee Grinder work?

    The grinding chamber is made up of two revolving and abrasive surfaces.

    One revolving surface spins whereas the other provides the needed support.

    This is the mechanism that eventually grinds the coffee.

    Why Conical Burr Coffee Grinder is useful and/or preferred ?

    Conical burr grinders are famous for the uniform texture of the final grinds.

    The slight uniformity in shape, allows all the coffee beans to be accessed equally.

    They are also better suited for the task of grinding.

    More Electric Coffee Grinder Types: Doser vs. Doserless

    Not all electric coffee grinders are the same.

    Doser Grinders

    A doser grinder is a kind of coffee grinder that comes along with an extra container, called a doser.

    This extra container is basically used to store the coffee that has already been ground.

    It makes the entire coffee grinding a clean and convenient undertaking.

    That’s because it prevents chinks or particles of coffee from falling off the floor unnecessarily.

    How does a Doser Grinder work?

    All the other components of the coffee grinder function the same way as in a doser grinder.

    The doser however functions slightly differently.

    This is, because it is further subdivided into pie-shaped segments which do rotate whenever a lever is pulled.

    New section moves over to the dispenser chute in order to deliver the ground coffee directly into the filter basket.

    Doserless Grinder

    A doserless coffee bean grinder grinds the coffee beans directly into a group handle.

    To you use these sorts of coffee grinders, you need to put some lower volume of coffee beans. At once.

    How does a Doserless Coffee Grinder work?

    It works pretty much the same as the other standard coffee grinders.

    All you need is to provide a container under it, to store the ground coffee.

    You can regulate the amount of coffee you wish to pour into the collecting container.

    You can do this by manipulating the power switch.

    Many people consider this as slightly inconvenient as compared to the doser grinders.

    Antique Coffee Grinder

    What sort of grinder is this Antique Coffee grinder?

    This is basically a coffee grinder that was used in ancient times.

    It is manual in the sense that human effort is what is used to power it.

    It comprises burrs rather than blades, is doserless, and stepless.

    How is this Antique Coffee grinder used?  

    The antique grinder has a wheel which connects to a burr.

    To grind your coffee, you simply need to turn this wheel.

    The motion that is derived from the action of turning the wheel is transmitted to the grinder.

    It is subsequently used to crush the coffee beans.

    Why do people love this Antique Coffee grinder?

    In ancient times, people had no choice but to utilize it because there were no alternatives.

    In modern times, most people like it because of its aesthetic value.

    They also prefer it because it requires no consumption of electricity and can also be used in a remote location.

    It is actually a memoir of sorts.

    What sort of grinder is this Wooden Coffee Grinder is?

    It has a burr which performs the actions of grinding coffee.

    It is stepless and but nonetheless has a doser.

    How is this Wooden Coffee grinder used?

    You are required to turn this wheel to provide a rotational force.

    This force is transmitted to the burr which subsequently grinds your coffee.

    Why do people love this Wooden Coffee grinder?

    Most people love it for its simplicity. They do not have to possess complex skills to understand how to use it.

    Do they need much cleaning or maintenance?

    YES, they do need much cleaning and maintenance.

    The following sections are particularly vulnerable to dirt and must hence be given much attention:

    Burr vs Blade

    These are the components that perform the actual task of grinding the coffee.

    They are susceptible to dirt and are thus very dangerous if not cleaned properly.

    They have to be cleaned after each grinding session and before use.

    Feeding Chute

    It is the feeding chute that holds the coffee beans prior to their crushing.

    They too may hold plenty of dirt and thus require constant cleaning.

    This is to prevent the coffee inside from being contaminated with dirt.

    Doser

    The doser stores the coffee that has already been ground.

    It may also accumulate plenty of dirt after several usages.

    For this reason, you will have to make appropriate arrangements to clean it up.

    Why shouldn’t you use a blade grinder for your coffee?

    Blade grinders have some severe disadvantages. Below are but a few of them:

    Noise

    The produce lots of noise compared to the burr grinders.

    This noise definitely interferes with those who are found in the surrounding areas.

    Because of this, you may not rely on the grinder for use in areas that are sensitive to noise.

    Texture

    Owing to the utilization of chopping to cut down the coffee beans to desired sizes, the texture of the final output is quite rough.

    The coffee achieved by blade grinders is undesirable for consumption.

    That’s because they scour the tongue and may also require you to spit every quite often.

    Grind Quality

    Each grain of coffee produced by blade grinders has unique sizes and shapes.

    This inconsistency leads to relatively lower grind quality compared mainly to those produced by the burr grinders.

    Heat

    Apart from sound, the blade grinders also generate plenty of heat.

    This heat, in turn, burns the little fat that is contained in the coffee beans.

    As such, it leads to a slight reduction of the quality of the final output.
