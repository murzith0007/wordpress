    Features And Benefits

    JavaPresse burr coffee grinder comes with many features, which is why it quickly becoming a staple in many households.

    I like that it is a slender machine and is therefore, easy to store in a cupboard or even toss in a suitcase for traveling.

    One of the best features of the small JavaPresse burr mill is its built-in grind selector.

    This allows you to choose between 18 click settings to grind your coffee with.

    If you are bored with a grinder that only allows you to pick from two different grinds, then this machine can make you happy!

    JavaPresse Coffee Grinder Parts

    The JavaPresse grinder comes with a stainless-steel lid and body, that the company claims are stronger than stainless steel.

     

    The lid ensures no spillage, and comes with a convenient hand crack that attaches to the top of the gadget.

     

    The crank allows the users to control how coarse they want their coffee to turn out, making it very user friendly and customizable.

    The crank is also quiet and does not create much noise.

    The unit also comes with a ceramic burr, that has recently been upgraded, and a sleek housing of the machine, as well.

     

    The upgraded burr is more durable and allows for less movement when the coffee grinder is in action.

     

    The unit itself comes in 4 pieces: bottom collection jar, top body where coffee beans go, the lid, and hand crank on top.

    The entire unit of the grinder is designed with convenience. It can easily be held, is lightweight, and can be stored in a bag or cupboard without taking up much room. 

    Ceramic Burr

    There are not many small pieces to the JavaPresse coffee mill.

    If you buy the manual grinder (or French Press), these are easy enough to disassemble!

     

    There is nothing more frustrating than standing at the kitchen counter hopelessly trying to piece back together your coffee grinder.

    The JavaPresse allows you to hand-grind your own coffee beans, which means you can customize every cup of coffee you sit down to enjoy.

    All you have to do is turn the knob to the right and the numbers below represent every click you make to the left.

    The number you choose to take will determine the coarseness of your coffee beans. 

    Take a look at the numbers:

    • Coarse: 13-18
    • Medium: 10-12
    • Medium Coarse: 7-9
    • Medium Fine: 4-6
    • Extra Fine: 1-3

    The more coffee you grind, the more you will see which type you prefer best.
