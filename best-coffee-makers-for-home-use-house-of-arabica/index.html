    Conclusion

    When buying a coffee maker, consider these top factors

    Cup Selector

    When picking out your coffee maker, keep in mind the number of cups you plan on brewing at once.

    If you have a large, caffeine-loving family, look for a machine that can easily brew multiple cups at once so that everyone can enjoy their java without delay.

    If it’s just you in your household, you can get away with a model that that produces smaller batches, such as a french press or a single-serving machine, like a Keurig.

    This will dictate what model you decide to buy.

    A large automatic drip maker might not be best for a solo drinker, for example (unless you like to store leftover coffee in the fridge.)

    Coffee Strength

    Some coffee makers have a programmable strength setting, but not all of them do.

    If you want a model that can produce a specific level, look for a machine that has a brew-strength control.

    This option is great if you consider yourself to have “refined” tastes or like to experiment with different settings.

    It is also super helpful if you have multiple coffee lovers in your household that prefer different strengths.

    With this feature, you can easily cater to everyone’s needs without too much hassle.

    However, if you care more about getting that liquid caffeine without any frills, this option probably doesn’t matter to you!

    Also, if everyone in your house prefers a similar strength level, then you will be fine looking for models without this feature.

    Timer and Auto-Shutoff

    Coffee makers with timers are perfect for daily caffeine drinkers!

    If you already know that you need your java at about the same time every single day, I recommend looking for a model that has this option.

    Set the timer, and you will have coffee waiting for you when you wake up.

    No need to worry about functioning in the morning when you’re under-caffeinated.

    The machine will do it for you.

    And I do admit, I love waking up to the smell of my java that has already been brewed.

    On that note, models with auto-shutoffs can be quite handy, as well.​

    I like models with this option because I get distracted easily in the mornings and I don’t always remember to turn off the machine!

    Have you ever accidentally left your brewer on, only to find that the hot plate has burnt the bottom of your carafe?

    Yeah. Me too.

    Grinder

    Coffee lovers generally agree that freshly-ground beans produce the best tasting drink.

    It’s all about personal preference, of course.

    That’s why many java drinkers choose coffee makers that have built-in grinders.

    This can be extra convenient, plus save space in your kitchen.

    Are you looking for quick, easy, and convenient?

    Or, are you looking for gourmet, specialty coffee?

    If you want something more specialized, consider purchasing a high-quality coffee grinder that is separate from your coffee maker for flexibility.

    Beverage of choice

    How do you like your coffee?

    This is undoubtedly one of the most important factors when selecting your coffee maker!

    Do you like good ol’ drip coffee?

    Is your priority a hands-off experience where all you have to do is press one button?

    Or, are you looking for butt-kicking Americanos and fluffy cappuccinos?

    All of these will require different models that have unique features.

    For example, if you are an espresso fiend, remember that espresso machines won’t be the same as your standard drip making models.

    Also consider whether or not you need a steam wand (this is used to make lattes and cappuccinos.)

    Carafe

    You have a couple of options here: your standard glass carafe or a thermal one.

    Thermal carafes are double-walled containers, usually made from stainless steel.

    Do you need to keep your coffee hot for long periods of time?

    Do you have a slightly bigger budget for your purchase?

    If, so, consider selecting a thermal model.

    Or, do you have a smaller budget?

    And are you planning on drinking your coffee right away?

    If you don’t need to maintain the heat and flavor of your coffee for several hours, a glass container is fine for you

    (but maybe not if you are a little clumsy! They can break easily! I learned this the hard way.)

    All Coffee Makers FAQs

    Below I’ll walk you through some of the more specific questions that you might have about coffeemakers.

    What coffee makers make the hottest coffee?

    Do you like your java especially hot?

    If that’s one of your biggest priorities, consider checking out Behmor Connected Customizable Temperature Control Coffee Maker.

    This one allows the ultimate flexibility for temperature control.

    It can brew at temps as high as 210 degrees Fahrenheit.

    Just keep in mind that normally the recommended maximum is between 95 and 205 degrees.

    How do clean your brewer if you don’t have vinegar?

    All out of vinegar but need to do some clean-up?

    Look for other acidic substitutes that you might already have in your kitchen, such as lemon or lime juice.

    The cleaning method will be just the same as with vinegar.

    You can also use baking soda.

    However, if you already have a lot of mineral build-up, this isn’t the best option.

    Look for dedicated coffee maker cleaners if you don’t want to use lemon juice or baking soda.

    These includes products such as Brew Rite Coffee Maker Cleaner.

    What if my coffee maker gets clogged?

    The last problem you feel like dealing with when you’re under-caffeinated is a clogged machine!

    Depending on the problem, it can be quite simple to fix the issue if you have the correct materials.

    First, clean out the coffee maker.

    Add 1 cup of white vinegar and water to the water reservoir and run the coffee maker.

    Repeat a couple times. Remember, if you don’t have vinegar, you can use lemon juice.

    Make sure the drain hole is not obstructed.

    You can use a toothpick or a wire to poke out any debris.

    I recommend regularly declogging your device.

    How much coffee do you use for 6 cups?

    You might be surprised to learn that a “coffee cup” is different from a regular cup.

    So let’s just get this clear: While 1 cup = 8 ounces, a coffee cup is about 6 ounces.

    Confusing, right?

    The standard is about 2 tablespoons of coffee grounds for 1 cup of coffee (6 ounces!)

    Keep in mind that those coffee scoops that often come with your beans are about 2 tablespoons.

    So, to summarize:

    • 1 coffee scoop for every 6 ounces (2 tablespoons.)
    • That means 6 coffee scoops for 6 cups (12 tablespoons, or ¾ cup).

    Much of it depends on your taste.

    If you like something stronger, experiment with adding more grounds!

    How many tablespoons for 10 cups?

    We can use what I described above to figure out much coffee to use for 10 cups.

    Like I said, it all depends on your taste preference.

    If we are using 1 coffee scoop per coffee cup, then it makes sense that we would use 10 scoops to brew 10 cups, right?

    A helpful thing to remember: if you usually drink a whole mug of coffee, that’s going to be 8 ounces, not 6.
