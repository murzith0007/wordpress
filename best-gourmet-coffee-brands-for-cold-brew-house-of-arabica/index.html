    Never Underestimate The Power of Coffee Bean

    If you’re serious about your cup of joe, you may know the two main types of beans: Arabica and Robusta.

    The best cold brew brands always use 100% Arabica beans.

    They are less acidic and have subtle but delicious flavors perfect for cold brew.

    Also, consider choosing single-origin beans instead of blends.

    You will experience fuller, clearer flavors if you are using this java making method.

    Drum vs. Air Roasting

    Did you know that there are two main methods of roasting coffee beans?

    Most companies choose to drum-roast their beans because this is cheaper and faster.

    Only about 1% of companies choose the air-roasting method 

    However, you need to look for air-roasted beans when choosing a brand to use for your cold brew.

    When air-roasting, processors carefully control the temperature and process.

    This makes sure that the heat is distributed evenly, for a smoother, less bitter taste!

    The Importance of Roast Levels

    When you walk into your local coffee shop, the barista probably asks you whether you want a light or dark roast.

    Many java lovers don’t realize that light, medium and dark all have unique benefits.

    Cold brew lovers generally agree that dark is the ideal roast level. Dark roast tends to have deep, chocolatey notes. 

    It just so happens the cold brew method is really good for extracting these types of rich flavors. 

    If you prefer the lighter, fruitier notes of light roast, you will have to steep your grounds much longer.
